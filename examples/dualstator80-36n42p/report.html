the machine

Architecture

A 36-slot / 42-pole fractional-slot concentrated winding: each tooth is one continuous spiral coil, phased and signed by the star-of-slots layout, giving a fundamental winding factor kw1 = 0.943. Two stator boards sandwich a single rotor disk and are wired in series, so their torques add at the same current. There is no iron: no cogging, no saturation, and little inductance -- that constraint surfaces in the drive section.

Can you actually drive it?

The part most PCB-motor write-ups skip: an air-core winding has tiny inductance, so a stock FOC drive's PWM turns into large ripple current. The gate compares worst-case ripple v_bus / (4·L·f_pwm) against a budget of 30% of the continuous current.

the fine print

Verdict

On the model's numbers, dualstator80-36n42p makes 20.5 mNm continuously (±30%) from 2.52 g of copper -- and driving it choke-free fails the ripple gate by 32x -- budget ~204 uH of external inductance per phase.

Model honesty: this is an analytical, feasibility-grade model — treat absolute torque as ±30%. The field solver is validated to <1% against closed-form solutions; the error budget is dominated by magnet Br tolerance and fringing, your actual assembled air gap (the single most sensitive parameter), and copper etching variation. Relative comparisons between designs are much better than ±30%. Calibrate against FEMM or a bench coil before committing money to a build.
