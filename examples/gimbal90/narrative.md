## The board this repo was built to reproduce

gimbal90 is not a synthetic demo — it is a real, shipped 90 mm gimbal-motor
stator that was designed, hand-tuned and ordered as Gerbers before this repo
existed. It's a 12-coil / 14-pole (12N14P) dual-stator machine: two of these
boards sandwich one magnet rotor, wired in series.

What makes it the reference example is the **fully-routed artwork**: every
coil-to-coil link, the WYE star and the phase leads are baked into the
footprint as net-bearing copper — the board arrives from the fab needing no
routing at all. The tabs variant adds M3 half-hole mounts, a connector tab,
and all-PTH terminals (3.0 mm pads, 0.85 mm drills) with the terminal cluster
rotated +14° to fit a hard 100x100 mm panel.

This page was generated by `pcb-motor showcase` from the same `motor.json`
that produced the shipped boards; the regenerated copper is regression-pinned
to the shipped `.kicad_mod` files (geometric difference: zero).
