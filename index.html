pcb-motor

Design a motor whose stator is a circuit board.

A coreless axial-flux motor where the stator is a PCB. This repo takes you from “roughly this much torque in roughly this much space” to a Gerber zip you can upload to a fab — with a Biot–Savart engine underneath and a feasibility gate that reports the choke question before you order boards, not after. These pages are what pcb-motor showcase generates: fully self-contained, every polygon and field sample computed from the design.

dualstator90-12n14p — the board that was fabricated →

A real 90 mm 12N14P dual-stator gimbal that went to fab (shipped as gimbal90): fully-routed copper (every coil link, star and lead baked in), back iron, M3 tab mounts, PTH terminals. This repo regenerates it coordinate-for-coordinate and plots Gerbers that match the manufactured board layer-for-layer.

dualstator140-24n28p — a 140 mm design, by an independent agent GATE: needs a choke →

A fresh agent, given only an application brief and the design skill, took the workflow end to end on its own: a 24N28P machine (later given a third magnet ring), ~173 mNm continuous (±30%), a complete board and fab-ready Gerbers — and an honest verdict that it needs a series choke to run from a bare ODrive.

dualstator80-36n42p — an 80 mm no-choke attempt, honestly lost GATE: FAIL ×32 →

42 poles of off-the-shelf disc magnets, two tapered-copper stators in series, 20.5 mNm continuous (±30%) — and the part the brief didn't want to hear, with the choke spec to fix it.
